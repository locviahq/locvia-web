Location Services Policy

Last updated: July 2026

Location is central to how Locvia works — it is what makes your marketplace hyperlocal rather than a generic nationwide listing site. This Policy explains, in plain terms, what location data we collect, why we collect it, how it is used, and what choices you have. It supplements, and should be read together with, our Privacy Policy.

01

Why Locvia Needs Your Location

Locvia calculates the distance between you and nearby listings to power your home feed, so that you primarily see items and services genuinely close to you (currently within an approximate 6-kilometer radius). We also use location to auto-fill your area, district, and state during signup and when you update your profile, saving you from typing this information manually.

02

When We Request Location Access

We request location permission during signup, when you tap "Update to my current location" in Edit Profile, and when you post a new listing. Location access on Android is requested through the standard system permission dialog, and you retain full control to allow, deny, or later revoke this permission through your device settings.

03

Precise vs. Approximate Location

Depending on the permission you grant and your device settings, Locvia may receive GPS-based precise coordinates or network/Wi-Fi-based approximate coordinates. Both are used solely to calculate distance for the nearby feed; we do not display your exact coordinates to other users at any precision level. Other users see only your general area and district, never a precise pinpoint or address.

04

What We Do With Location Data

Location coordinates are stored in association with your user account and, separately, with each listing you post (reflecting your location at the time of posting). This data is used to: filter and sort the nearby feed for you and other users; determine which nearby users receive a notification when you post a new listing; and support customer support or safety investigations where location context is relevant to a report.

05

Background Location

Locvia does not continuously track your location in the background. Location is captured only at the specific moments described in Section 2 — it is not polled continuously while the app is closed or running in the background.

06

If You Deny or Revoke Location Access

If you do not grant location permission, or later revoke it through your device settings, you can still use Locvia by manually selecting your state and district from the app's dropdown menus. In this case, your nearby feed will be based on your selected district rather than a precise 6-kilometer radius, which may show listings from a broader area. You can re-enable location access, or use "Update to my current location" in Edit Profile, at any time to switch back to precise distance-based filtering.

07

Location Accuracy for Listings

When you post a listing, your account's current location coordinates (if available) are attached to that listing so that other users can see accurate distance from their own location. If you move to a new area, we recommend updating your location in Edit Profile so that future listings reflect your current location accurately.

08

Third-Party Processing of Location Data

Location coordinates you provide are stored using Google Firebase's Cloud Firestore database, and distance calculations are performed by our application logic. We do not share your location data with any advertising network or data broker. See our Privacy Policy for further detail on our use of Google Firebase.

09

Children and Location

As Locvia is intended for users 18 years and older, location data is not knowingly collected from minors. See our Privacy Policy for our children's privacy commitments.

10

Changes to This Policy

We may update this Policy as our location features evolve — for example, if we introduce more granular radius controls or new location-based features. Material changes will be communicated through the app.

11

Contact Us
