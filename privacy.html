Legal

Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how Locvia Technologies ("Locvia", "we", "us", "our") collects, uses, discloses, retains, and protects information when you use the Locvia mobile application and any related services (collectively, the "Service"). This Policy is designed to comply with applicable data protection laws, including India's Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023 ("DPDP Act"), the Information Technology Act, 2000 and its rules, the EU General Data Protection Regulation ("GDPR") for users in the European Economic Area, and the California Consumer Privacy Act ("CCPA") for California residents, to the extent applicable to our operations. By creating an account or otherwise using the Service, you acknowledge that you have read and understood this Policy.

01

Definitions

"Personal Data" means any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person. "Processing" means any operation performed on Personal Data, including collection, storage, use, disclosure, or deletion. "User", "you" refers to any individual who accesses or uses the Service. "Listing" means any item, service, or content posted by a user on the Service. "Third-Party Service Provider" means any external service (such as Google Firebase) that processes data on our behalf.

02

Information We Collect

2.1 Information You Provide Directly

  • Phone Number — collected and verified via One-Time Password (OTP) at signup, used as your primary account identifier.
  • Profile Information — your name, profile photo (if provided), and self-reported area, district, and state.
  • Listing Content — titles, descriptions, prices, categories, and photographs you upload when creating a listing.
  • Communications — messages you send through in-app chat with other users.
  • Reports and Feedback — information you submit when reporting a user, listing, or technical problem.

2.2 Information Collected Automatically

  • Location Data — with your permission, we collect approximate or precise GPS coordinates to calculate distance for our hyperlocal ("nearby") feed and to auto-fill your area and district. You may deny or later revoke this permission through your device settings.
  • Device and Usage Information — device model, operating system version, app version, crash logs, and general usage patterns (such as screens visited and features used), collected for analytics and app stability purposes.
  • IP Address — collected incidentally by our infrastructure providers as part of standard network communication and security monitoring; we do not use IP address as a primary means of identifying you.

2.3 Information We Do Not Collect

We do not access your device contacts, call logs, SMS messages (other than the automatic OTP retrieval permitted by Android for authentication), camera roll beyond images you explicitly select to upload, or any biometric data. If any future feature requires additional categories of data, we will update this Policy and, where legally required, obtain your fresh consent before collection begins.

03

How We Use Your Information

We Process Personal Data for the following purposes: (a) to create and authenticate your account via OTP; (b) to operate the hyperlocal marketplace, including displaying listings within your configured radius; (c) to enable in-app communication between buyers and sellers while shielding your phone number from other users; (d) to send you transactional and service notifications (such as new messages or nearby listings), subject to your notification preferences; (e) to investigate reports of abuse, fraud, or policy violations; (f) to maintain, debug, and improve the Service through aggregated or de-identified analytics; and (g) to comply with legal obligations, enforce our Terms & Conditions, and protect the rights, property, and safety of Locvia, our users, and the public.

04

Legal Basis for Processing (GDPR)

For users in jurisdictions requiring a legal basis for Processing, we rely on: performance of a contract (to provide the Service you signed up for); legitimate interests (to secure, maintain, and improve the Service, and to prevent fraud), balanced against your rights; consent (for optional features such as precise location or marketing communications, where required); and legal obligation (where Processing is necessary to comply with applicable law).

05

How We Share Your Information

We do not sell your Personal Data. We do not display your phone number to other users under any circumstance — all buyer-seller communication occurs through in-app chat. Your first name and general area (not your precise address) are visible on your public listings and profile. We share information only in the following circumstances:

  • With Third-Party Service Providers who process data on our behalf under contractual confidentiality and security obligations, including: Google Firebase (Authentication, Cloud Firestore database, Cloud Storage for photos, Cloud Messaging for push notifications, Analytics, and Crashlytics for crash reporting) and Google Cloud Platform (hosting infrastructure).
  • With law enforcement, regulators, or courts where disclosure is required by applicable law, legal process, or to protect the rights, safety, or property of Locvia, our users, or the public.
  • In connection with a merger, acquisition, financing, or sale of assets, in which case we will notify you of any change in ownership or use of your Personal Data.
  • With your explicit consent, for any purpose not otherwise described in this Policy.
06

Third-Party Services

Locvia is built on Google Firebase and Google Play Services. These providers may independently collect certain technical data (such as device identifiers used for push notification delivery) under their own privacy terms, available at Google's Privacy Policy (policies.google.com/privacy). Our use of Google Play Services is also subject to Google Play's Developer Distribution Agreement. We select service providers who maintain appropriate technical and organizational security measures, but we do not control their independent data practices beyond our contractual instructions to them.

07

Location Services

Location access is fundamental to Locvia's hyperlocal marketplace model and is used to: (a) determine listings and users within your configured radius (currently approximately 6 kilometers); (b) auto-populate your area, district, and state during signup and profile edits; and (c) improve the relevance of notifications about nearby listings. Location data is stored as coordinates associated with your account and, where you post a listing, with that listing. We do not continuously track your location in the background outside of active use of location-dependent features. You may deny location permission or use manual state/district selection as an alternative; doing so may reduce the accuracy of nearby listing recommendations. See our standalone Location Services Policy for further detail.

08

Data Retention

We retain your Personal Data for as long as your account remains active and as necessary to provide the Service. If you delete your account through Settings, your account enters a 30-day grace period during which it is deactivated but recoverable by logging back in. After 30 days, we permanently delete your profile, listings, and message content from active systems, except where retention is required to comply with a legal obligation, resolve disputes, enforce our agreements, or as reflected in routine backup cycles, which are purged on a rolling basis. Aggregated or de-identified data that can no longer be linked to you may be retained indefinitely for analytics and product improvement.

09

Your Rights

Subject to applicable law, you have the right to: access the Personal Data we hold about you; correct inaccurate or incomplete data (via Edit Profile); request deletion of your account and associated data (via Settings → Delete Account, or see our Delete Account page); withdraw consent for optional Processing (such as location access) at any time through your device settings, without affecting the lawfulness of Processing before withdrawal; object to or request restriction of certain Processing; and, where applicable under GDPR, request data portability. EEA and UK residents additionally have the right to lodge a complaint with their local data protection supervisory authority. To exercise any of these rights, contact us using the details in Section 15.

10

Data Security

We implement industry-standard technical and organizational safeguards to protect your Personal Data, including encryption of data in transit (TLS) and at rest via our infrastructure provider, access controls limiting internal data access to authorized personnel on a need-to-know basis, and phone number verification via OTP to reduce account takeover risk. No method of electronic transmission or storage is completely secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security. You are responsible for maintaining the confidentiality of any device or session used to access your account.

11

Children's Privacy

The Service is not directed to, and we do not knowingly collect Personal Data from, individuals under the age of 18 (or the age of legal majority in your jurisdiction, if higher). If we become aware that we have inadvertently collected Personal Data from a minor, we will take reasonable steps to delete such data promptly. Parents or guardians who believe a minor has created an account should contact us immediately using the details in Section 15.

12

Law Enforcement and Legal Requests

We may disclose Personal Data in response to a valid legal request (such as a subpoena, court order, or government demand) where we have a good-faith belief that disclosure is required by law, or where necessary to protect the rights, property, or safety of Locvia, our users, or the public, including in cases of suspected fraud, harassment, or illegal activity. Where legally permitted, we will attempt to notify the affected user before disclosure. We may retain content and account information related to an active investigation or report beyond our standard retention period to preserve evidence.

13

International Data Transfers

Our infrastructure providers may process and store data on servers located outside your country of residence, including in the United States, in accordance with their published data center locations. Where we transfer Personal Data of EEA, UK, or Swiss residents outside those regions, we rely on appropriate safeguards recognized under GDPR, such as Standard Contractual Clauses, to the extent applicable through our infrastructure providers' compliance frameworks.

14

Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices, the Service, or applicable law. Material changes will be communicated through the app or, where required by law, by obtaining renewed consent. The "Last updated" date at the top of this Policy indicates when it was last revised. Continued use of the Service after changes take effect constitutes your acceptance of the revised Policy.

15

Contact Us

For any questions, concerns, or requests regarding this Privacy Policy or our data practices, contact us at hello.locvia@gmail.com. We aim to respond to all privacy-related inquiries within a reasonable timeframe, consistent with applicable law.
